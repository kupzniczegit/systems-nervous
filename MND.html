MND

Motor Neurone Disease

Motor Neurone disease (MND) is a rare neurodegenerative condition that progressively damages parts of the nervous system. 

MND is directly caused by motor neurons that stop working properly and die prematurely (that process is called neurodegeneration). These neurons are in charge of muscle activity like gripping, walking, speaking, swallowing or breathing.

The symptoms tend to appear on one side of the body, they get progressively worse and take over the other side. Early symptoms include limb-onset disease, bulbar-onset disease and respiratory-onset disease. Later on, the symptoms get more advanced and they will include increasing body paralysis and in some cases insomnia, anxiety, or depression.
