Epilepsy

Epilepsy is a chronic brain disorder characterized by seizures. it is estimated that 1 in 26 people develop this disorder and it affects all genders, races, ethnic backgrounds and ages.

Risk factors may include age (most common in children and older adults) and family history, but may also include head injuries and brain infections, or trauma. Epilepsy can also be a result of strokes. It is also worth adding that a person with epilepsy can both be caused by dementia or could cause it in the future.

This condition will vary widely. Some may lose awareness during a seizure and some might not. Others might repeatedly twitch their arms or legs (also known as convulsions or spasms) and some might not move at all.

Treatment with medicine or surgery can control seizures but it mostly depends on the person. It should also be mentioned that kids with epilepsy may outgrow their condition with age
