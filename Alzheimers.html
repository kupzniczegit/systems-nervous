Alzheimer's disease

Alzheimer’s disease is a neurodegenerative brain disorder characterized by slowly destroying one's memory, thinking skills, and eventually the ability to carry out the simplest tasks. It is the most common variant of dementia, making up 60-80% of cases.

One of the greatest known risk factors is increasing age, however it is not a normal part of aging. Other risk factors include being female, having diabetes, or just having a family history of the condition. It is believed that in order to slow down the development of this disease, a person should engage in logical games like crosswords or Sudoku on a daily basis to keep the brain functioning.

Because Alzheimer’s is a progressive disease, it worsens over time. In its early stages, memory loss is mild, but with late-stage Alzheimer’s, the person loses the ability to have a conversation and respond to the environment. On average, a person with Alzheimer’s lives 4 to 8 years after diagnosis but can live as long as 20 years, depending on outside factors.
